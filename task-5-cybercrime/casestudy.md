# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

**Cybercrime Type:** UPI / Online Payment Fraud
**Classification:** Financial Cybercrime

---

## Background

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) has transformed digital payments in India, with over 10 billion transactions processed monthly. However, its widespread adoption has also made it a prime target for cybercriminals who exploit users' unfamiliarity with the technology, particularly first-time users such as students and senior citizens.

---

## The Scenario

Riya, a first-year college student from Bhopal, recently moved into a hostel. She posted on a local Facebook group asking if anyone was selling a second-hand study table. A person responded quickly, saying they had one for ₹800 and offered to deliver it the same day.

The seller said they would send the table only after receiving an advance payment via UPI. They shared a QR code and asked Riya to scan it. When Riya scanned the code and entered her UPI PIN, instead of sending ₹800, she unknowingly **accepted a collect request** — and ₹800 was debited from her account. The seller immediately became unreachable.

---

## How It Happened (Step-by-Step)

1. **Target identification:** The fraudster identified Riya as a likely target — a new student, unfamiliar with the area, looking for cheap items.
2. **Trust building:** Polite, prompt responses built false confidence.
3. **Urgency creation:** A limited-time offer pressured Riya to act quickly without thinking.
4. **QR code trick:** The fraudster sent a **collect/payment request QR code** (not a receive-money QR code). Scanning it to "send" money actually authorised a debit.
5. **PIN exploitation:** Riya's UPI PIN completed the transaction, which is irreversible.
6. **Disappearance:** Contact was cut off immediately after the transaction succeeded.

---

## Who Is Targeted?

- First-time UPI users (students, elderly people)
- People buying/selling on informal platforms (OLX, Facebook Marketplace, local WhatsApp groups)
- People responding to "too good to be true" deals

---

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss (often ₹500 – ₹50,000)
- Psychological distress, embarrassment, and loss of trust in digital platforms
- Difficulty recovering money — UPI transactions are real-time and difficult to reverse
- In larger scams, victims may lose life savings or fall into debt
