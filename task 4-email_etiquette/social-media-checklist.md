# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do | Why It Matters |
|---|-----|----------------|
| 1 | **Keep your profiles professional and consistent** | Recruiters and internship coordinators routinely check LinkedIn and GitHub. A polished, consistent presence builds credibility. |
| 2 | **Use privacy settings on personal accounts** | Restrict who can see your personal posts, stories, and photos on Instagram, Snapchat, and Facebook. Not everything needs to be public. |
| 3 | **Think before you post** | Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" If no, don't post it. |
| 4 | **Give credit when sharing others' content** | Always tag the original creator or source when sharing articles, images, or ideas. It's both ethical and professional. |
| 5 | **Report cyberbullying and harassment** | Use in-platform reporting tools immediately if you or someone you know is being targeted. You can also report to cybercrime.gov.in or call 1930. |
| 6 | **Separate personal and professional accounts** | Use one set of profiles for professional networking (LinkedIn, GitHub) and keep personal accounts private or minimal. |
| 7 | **Fact-check before sharing news or information** | Misinformation spreads quickly. Verify from at least two reliable sources before forwarding anything, especially during elections or health emergencies. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Don't | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|----------------|
| 1 | **Don't share your OTP, password, or banking details online** | Legitimate organisations never ask for OTPs or passwords. Sharing them can lead to financial fraud. |
| 2 | **Don't post your live location or travel plans publicly** | Sharing your exact location or announcing you are away from home is a safety risk. |
| 3 | **Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying** | Participating in trolling or staying silent when others are being bullied is harmful. Speak up and report. |
| 4 | **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers** | Unknown accounts may be bots, scammers, or people with harmful intent. |
| 5 | **Don't share exam papers, internal documents, or confidential college information** | Sharing restricted academic content online may violate institutional policies and academic integrity rules. |
| 6 | **Don't use your professional profiles (LinkedIn/GitHub) for personal venting** | Keep complaints, political arguments, and personal drama off your professional platforms. |
| 7 | **Don't fall for "urgent" messages asking you to click a link or share information** | These are often phishing attempts. Verify through official channels before acting. |
